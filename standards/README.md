# Standards submission dossier

This directory collects the documents you need to move EtereCitizen from "open-source protocol" into the formal standards ecosystem — W3C, DIF, Ethereum ERC, and others — without paying for any of them.

Everything in here is drafted as-of 2026-04-21. Review, adjust, and submit manually; submission forms ask for answers only the project lead can give (affiliation, IPR agreement, contact channels).

## Recommended submission order

| Order | Submission | Cost | Timeline | Document |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **W3C Community Group** proposal | Free | ~1 week for approval after charter + 5 supporters | [w3c-cg-charter.md](./w3c-cg-charter.md) + [w3c-cg-submission-guide.md](./w3c-cg-submission-guide.md) |
| 2 | **DIF Associate Membership** (individual tier) | Free | Immediate | [dif-membership.md](./dif-membership.md) |
| 3 | **did:ethr Registry note** updating the existing registration with EtereCitizen usage | Free | PR to w3c/did-spec-registries | [did-methods-registry-note.md](./did-methods-registry-note.md) |
| 4 | **arXiv paper** draft | Free | 2-4 weeks writing | [arxiv-paper-outline.md](./arxiv-paper-outline.md) |

## Why these four

- **W3C CG** = legitimacy badge that every serious protocol adopter looks for. The Community Groups page shows "EtereCitizen — Agent Identity and Trust CG" alongside Solid, DID WG, VC WG, etc.
- **DIF** = network effects. DIF hosts the decentralized-identity ecosystem. Being a named associate puts EtereCitizen into the weekly working-group conversations where interop happens.
- **DID Method Registry** note = discoverability for anyone surveying agent-identity approaches.
- **arXiv** = academic citeability. "As described in Trani 2026 [arxiv link]" is a line you can put in slides.

## What I've drafted vs what you provide

The markdown in this directory is substantively complete. You plug in:

- Your legal / preferred name as contact in each submission form.
- Organization affiliation (if any).
- The initial list of supporters for the W3C CG (5 people is enough; they can be anyone with a W3C account — free).
- Meeting time preferences.

After that, every submission is a copy-paste of the drafted text into the relevant form.
