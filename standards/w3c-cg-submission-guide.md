# W3C Community Group — submission walkthrough

Practical, click-by-click guide to submitting the [Agent Identity and Trust CG](./w3c-cg-charter.md) proposal to W3C. Total time: ~45 minutes once supporters are lined up.

## Prerequisites

- **A W3C account.** Free, individual-tier. Sign up at https://www.w3.org/accounts/request.
- **5 supporters with W3C accounts.** They don't need to represent any company. Ask them privately before submission; you'll enter their email addresses on the form.
- **A few hours of supporter availability in the 7 days after submission.** W3C sometimes emails supporters to confirm they're real.

## Step 1 — Ensure supporters are ready

Send this template to 5 people (colleagues, collaborators, open-source peers):

> Hi — I'm submitting a W3C Community Group proposal for agent-identity standards (name: "Agent Identity and Trust CG"). Community Groups need 5 public supporters with W3C accounts. Would you be willing to be listed? It's ~30 seconds of your time (sign in to W3C, click "Support"). The charter is at [link to standards/w3c-cg-charter.md on GitHub].

Do this BEFORE submitting.

## Step 2 — Open the proposal form

Go to https://www.w3.org/community/groups/propose/ (must be signed in).

## Step 3 — Fill the form

Map the charter sections to form fields as follows:

| Form field | From charter section | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Name | §1 | Exactly "Agent Identity and Trust Community Group" |
| URL slug | — | Suggest "agent-identity" |
| Short description | §2 | Copy verbatim, one paragraph |
| Mission | §3 first 3 bullet points | Keep under 500 words |
| Deliverables | §4 | Copy items 1-4, drop stretch goals for now |
| Chairs | §8 | Your name + W3C username |

Upload the full charter (`w3c-cg-charter.md`) as a supporting document if the form has an attach field. Otherwise link to the GitHub copy.

## Step 4 — Submit

Click "Submit". The W3C team reviews within ~1 week. They may:

- **Approve** — the group goes public and you can start recruiting.
- **Request revisions** — usually minor (clarify scope, adjust deliverables).
- **Decline** — rare for CGs; usually only if the scope duplicates an existing group.

## Step 5 — Recruit supporters

After approval, share the CG URL (provisioned by W3C) on:

- Twitter / X (your handle, tag @w3c).
- Fediverse (Mastodon).
- Ethereum community forums (Ethereum Magicians, ERC channels for ERC-8004 folks).
- LinkedIn.
- DIF Slack (apply to join — see [dif-membership.md](./dif-membership.md)).
- r/ethereum, r/selfsovereign, HN "Show HN" (cautiously — audience fit matters).

Your pitch is the charter §5 Rationale.

## Step 6 — First meeting

Schedule the kickoff for ~2 weeks after approval. Agenda template:

1. Introductions (5 min)
2. Charter walkthrough and open discussion (20 min)
3. Seed material overview — present EtereCitizen v0.3 SPEC.md (15 min)
4. Editorial process: GitHub-first, Docusaurus site, conformance vectors (10 min)
5. Open questions / roadmap for next 3 meetings (10 min)

Post minutes publicly. Keep them terse.

## Common traps to avoid

- **Do NOT claim the Group produces W3C Recommendations.** CGs produce CG Reports. Recommendations require a Working Group (which is a bigger commitment — charter, IPR, membership dues).
- **Do NOT lock the scope to EtereCitizen.** The CG should be able to evolve the spec. If the Group concludes a different approach is better, that's fine.
- **Do NOT promise timelines.** CGs operate as best-effort. "First draft in Q3 2026" is OK; "v1 ratified in 2026" is not.

## After the first 6 months

If the CG has active participants and a clear spec trajectory, consider proposing a formal Working Group. That's a bigger step (W3C membership fees for at least one sponsor) but converts the CG Report into a candidate Recommendation.

Until then, the CG Report is itself a recognised standards artefact — cite it, link to it, get implementations to claim conformance.
